Republic of Congo

The Republic of the Congo, often referred to as Congo-Brazzaville, is a Central African country located across the Equator. Characterized by its low population density, it has vast tropical forest cover that forms part of the world’s second-largest forest basin, the Congo Basin. Its economy relies heavily on oil production, which accounts for most of its GDP and exports, giving it relative prosperity. However, this wealth is often challenged by unequal distribution, economic instability, and persistent social issues such as a high poverty rate, particularly in rural areas. Its capital, Brazzaville, and the port city of Pointe-Noire are home to most of its population.
